ELEPHANT

An elephant is the largest land animal on Earth. It has a long trunk, large ears, and tusks made of ivory. Elephants are intelligent, social animals that live in groups called herds. They mainly eat grass, leaves, and fruits. There are three species — the African savanna elephant, African forest elephant, and Asian elephant. Elephants are known for their strong memory and are considered symbols of wisdom and strength.

FOX

A fox is a small, clever mammal known for its pointed ears, bushy tail, and sharp eyes. It belongs to the dog family (Canidae). Foxes are omnivores, eating small animals, fruits, and insects. They are mainly nocturnal and live in burrows. The most common species is the red fox, found in many parts of the world. Foxes are often seen as smart and cunning animals in stories and folklore.

BEAR

A bear is a large, strong mammal with thick fur, a short tail, and sharp claws. Bears are omnivores, eating plants, fish, and small animals. They live in forests, mountains, and cold regions. Common types include the brown bear, polar bear, and black bear. Bears are known for their strength and sometimes hibernate during winter.

WOLFs

A wolf is a wild, carnivorous animal and the largest member of the dog family (Canidae). It has sharp teeth, strong jaws, and thick fur. Wolves live and hunt in groups called packs. They communicate by howling and are known for their teamwork and loyalty. The gray wolf is the most common species.

TIGER

A tiger is a large, powerful wild cat known for its orange coat with black stripes. It is a carnivore that hunts animals like deer and wild boar. Tigers live mostly in forests and grasslands across Asia. They are solitary animals and excellent swimmers. The tiger is a symbol of strength and courage.

LION

A lion is a large, powerful wild cat known as the “king of the jungle.” Male lions have a thick mane around their head. They live in groups called prides and mainly eat meat. Lions are found mostly in Africa and parts of India. They are symbols of bravery, strength, and leadership.
